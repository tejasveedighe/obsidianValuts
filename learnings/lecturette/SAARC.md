1. Introduction
	- SAARC -> South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation
	- It is a inter-governmental organization or association and union of nations that was founded in 1985 in Dhaka (Bangladesh).
	- Headquarters - Kathmandu, [[Nepal]]
	- Members 8 - [[Afghanistan]], [[Pakistan]], [[India]], [[Sri Lanka]], [[Nepal]], [[Bangladesh]], [[Bhutan]], [[Maldives]].
	- Created for -
		- Improving trade and relationship
		- Promote peace, stability in economic cooperation
		- Tackle socio-economic challenges like Poverty, Illiteracy and healthcare.
2. Current Scenario
	- Initiatives  -
		- SAFTA (South Asian Free Trade Area)
			- Aims to improve intra-regional trade
		- SAARC Disaster Management Centre 
		- SAARC Development Fund
			- Supports various regional projects.
		- SAARC University
			- Established in India for academic collaboration.
	- Challenges - 
		- Political tensions between members make it difficult for collaboration on confliction interests.
		- The financial integration and infrastructure needs to be worked upon.
		- The agreements need to be more efficiently implemented.
3. Conclusion -
	- SAARC is a organization that could benefit the members in collaborating to bring the benefit to their citizens.
	- SAARC has brought many initiatives like free trade zone to improve the trading between its members and is a good initiative to improve together.
	- Helping each other and working towards mutual goals will help the region to develop and lead to overall development for human kind.
