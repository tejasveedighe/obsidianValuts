1. Introduction of the topic
	- The bill is introduced in the parliament to regulate the entry, stay and exit of foreigners in India.
	- The bill repeals outdated laws that allowed entry to India.
		-  The Passport (Entry into India) Act, 1920
		- The Registration of Foreigners Act, 1939
		- The Foreigners Act, 1946
		- The Immigration (Carriersâ€™ Liability) Act, 2000
	- The bill proposes a Bureau of Immigration to oversee the issuance of Visas and checking of documents of any foreigner before the entry to India. A commissioner of Central Government will oversee the bureau. 
	- The bill makes it mandatory for any foreigner to hold a valid passport to enter India.
	- The bill has changed the framework of visas, adding a startup visa to attract foreign investments in India. Education visas also need to submit the information of the individual to the Regional offices, before admitting a foreign student.
	- The bill also introduces penalties to violation of the rules of entry, stay and exit from India.

2. Current Scenario
	- The bill has been passed by Lok Sabha in March 2025 and Rajya Sabha in April.
	- It is still awaiting presidential approval.

3. Conclusion
	- The Bill will prove to be very beneficial for the people of India, as it repels the illegal immigration.
	- The Bill will make it easier for other nation to invest in India, ultimately improving the cross border relations.
	- The Bill will make it easier as a specific bureau will oversee the movement and documentation of foreigners.
	- Also will be adding more jobs for such offices. More growth for the youth.
