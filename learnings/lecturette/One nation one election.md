- Introduction 
	- One nation and one election is a proposal that is under consideration by the Government of India.
	- It aims to make the elections one one day and one time with the goal to reduce the election costs.
	- The Bill was introduced during the election of Lok Sabha in December of 2024.
- How elections are held in India:
	- There are three bodies of the Parliament for which the elections are held- 
		1. [[Lok Sabha]] (Lower House/General) -
			1. Every 5 years, the elections of a constituency is held in similar time frame or within a few days.
			2. These are held over a few weeks due to large number of voters.
			3. 2024 elections were held for 44 days.
		2. [[Rajya Sabha]] (Upper House/Council Of States) - Every 2 years, the members are retired after 2 years.
		3. [[Vidhan Sabha]] (State) - Every 5 years, for state legistatives.
		4. Local Body Elections - Urban and Rural local body election are held with varying frequencies.
	- [[Election Commission of India]] - 
		- The ECI is responsible for administration of all the elections held in India. 
		- It ensures that elections are free and fair.
	- Voting Process - 
		- [[Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs)]] - 
			- Used for recording the votes
		- Voter Verified Paper Audit Trail (VVPAT) - 
			- for enhancing transparency
		- Polling Stations - 
			- These are stations that are close to people to ensure that every person gets to vote. It is typically a km from a person.
- Costs of elections - 
	- 2024 Lok sabha elections estimated a total cost of Rs.1.35 lakh crore. It was the most expensive election in Indian History.
	- The one nation one election would make the cost to 7000 crores, that means more fund for welfare and development.
