- Introduction
	- G20 is a group of 19 countries including the European union and the African union.
	- G20 works on major issues related to global economy. Issues such as financial stability, climate change mitigation, and sustainable development, via annual meetings of head of states.
	- G20 was founded in 1999, due to economic problems.
	- G20 makes up to 85 % of Global GDP
		- 75 % of international trade
		- And two thirds of the global population
	- Each year a annual meet is set up with different member as Host of the meet, last year 2024 the G20 Summit was held at Rio de Janeiro, Brazil.
		- The host is decided based on the turn of groups, the 20 members are divided in to 5 groups based on the area, countries of similar region are placed in one group.
		- In 2024 the region was South American group.
		- The group negotiates among themselves and selects a chair and host for the meet.
	- India held the 2023 presidency, the theme of summit was Vasudhaiva Kutumbakum - meaning One Earth, One family, One future.
		- Various issues like
			- Climate action and sustainability
			- Digital public infrastructure
			- Women empowerment
			- Reforming global institutions for WTO and IMF
